A Jet Stream B Thermohaline circulation C El Niño-Southern Oscillation D Trade Winds

A Tundra B Mangroves C Grasslands D Deserts

A Ecosystem-based adaptation B Structural adaptation C Non-structural adaptation D Behavioral adaptation
What is the process by which water-saturated ground loses strength and stiffness, often leading to landslides and building collapse during flooding?
A Sedimentation B Erosion C Liquefaction D Subsidence

A World Meteorological Organization (WMO) B United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) C Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPC) D International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)

A Groundwater extraction B Thermal expansion C Ice sheet dynamics D Ocean circulation changes
What term describes the ability of a system, community, or society to withstand and recover from climate-related impacts, including flooding?
A Sustainability B Resilience C Adaptation D Mitigation

A Pacific Decadal Oscillation B North Atlantic Oscillation C Madden-Julian Oscillation D Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation
What is the primary human-induced cause of the increasing frequency of extreme weather events, including heavy rainfall and flooding?

A Carbon sequestration B Water filtration C Habitat provision D Flood regulation

A Erosion B Sedimentation C Subsidence D Liquefaction
What type of flooding occurs when heavy rainfall exceeds the absorption capacity of the soil, leading to water flowing over the land surface?
A Coastal flooding B River flooding C Pluvial flooding D Groundwater flooding
Which international framework aims to integrate disaster risk reduction into sustainable development policies and planning?
A Kyoto Protocol B Sendai Framework C Paris Agreement D Cancun Adaptation Framework
